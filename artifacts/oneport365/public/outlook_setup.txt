HOW TO CONNECT YOUR COMPANY MICROSOFT EMAIL
OnePort 365 — Email Monitoring Setup
==========================================

OVERVIEW
--------
  Company emails hosted on Microsoft 365 (e.g. yourname@oneport365.com,
  yourname@company.com) can connect to OnePort 365 using an App Password.
  This requires your Microsoft 365 account to have Multi-Factor
  Authentication (MFA) enabled — which most company accounts already have.


STEP 1 — CONFIRM MFA IS ENABLED ON YOUR ACCOUNT
-------------------------------------------------
  1. Go to: https://mysignins.microsoft.com/security-info
  2. Sign in with your company Microsoft account
  3. You should see at least one authentication method listed
     (e.g. Authenticator app, phone number)
  4. If MFA is not set up, ask your IT administrator to enable it —
     App Passwords are only available once MFA is active


STEP 2 — CREATE AN APP PASSWORD
---------------------------------
  1. Go to: https://account.microsoft.com
  2. Sign in with your COMPANY email address and password
  3. Click Security (top navigation)
  4. Click Advanced security options
  5. Scroll to "App passwords" and click Create a new app password
  6. Name it "OnePort 365" and click Next
  7. Copy the generated password immediately — you won't see it again

  IMPORTANT: If you do not see the "App passwords" option:
    → Your IT administrator may have disabled App Passwords at the tenant
      level. Contact IT and ask them to enable "App Passwords" for your
      account in the Microsoft 365 admin portal (under Azure AD →
      Multi-factor authentication settings → Allow users to create
      app passwords).


STEP 3 — ENABLE IMAP IN OUTLOOK (if not already on)
-----------------------------------------------------
  Most company accounts have IMAP enabled by default. If the connection
  test fails, your IT admin can confirm or enable it:

  IT Admin steps (Microsoft 365 Admin Center):
    1. Go to admin.microsoft.com
    2. Users → Active users → select the user
    3. Mail tab → Manage email apps
    4. Tick "IMAP" → Save

  Individual check (Outlook Web):
    1. Go to https://outlook.office.com
    2. Settings (gear icon) → View all Outlook settings
    3. Mail → Sync email → POP and IMAP
    4. Confirm IMAP is enabled


STEP 4 — CONNECT IN ONEPORT 365
---------------------------------
  1. Log in to mvp1.html
  2. Click the envelope icon in the top navigation bar
  3. Open Email Monitoring settings
  4. Click the Outlook button in "Connect a new inbox"
  5. Enter your full company email (e.g. yourname@company.com)
  6. Paste the App Password from Step 2
  7. Click "Test connection"
     - Success: "Connected — X unread in INBOX"
     - Failure: see Troubleshooting below
  8. Click "Connect inbox" to save and start syncing


TROUBLESHOOTING
---------------
  "Authentication failed"
    → You used your regular password instead of the App Password
    → Re-generate an App Password (Step 2) and try again
    → MFA may not be enabled — confirm with IT

  "App passwords option not visible in Microsoft account"
    → IT has not enabled App Passwords for your organisation
    → Ask IT to allow App Passwords in: Azure AD → MFA settings

  "Connection failed" or timeout
    → IMAP may be disabled on your mailbox — ask IT to enable it (Step 3)

  "This feature is not available for your account"
    → Your Microsoft 365 tenant may enforce Conditional Access policies
      that block basic auth entirely. This is common in high-security
      environments. Ask IT to either:
        a) Create an exception for IMAP App Passwords for your account, or
        b) Confirm which monitored inbox address can be used


WHAT YOUR IT ADMINISTRATOR NEEDS TO KNOW
-----------------------------------------
  OnePort 365 connects via:
    Protocol : IMAP over SSL/TLS
    Host     : imap.outlook.com  (Microsoft 365 / Exchange Online)
    Port     : 993
    Auth     : Username (company email) + App Password (MFA-backed)

  No data leaves your inbox — the system only reads subject, sender,
  and body of incoming emails to extract freight RFQ information.
  Credentials are stored encrypted in the OnePort 365 database.
